# Overview:
## Initial Description:
Shaped like two crescent moons with concave curves facing each other, Horny Island is so named for its resemblance to a pair of horns, and no other reason. Across each opening to the inner bay stretch two gates that can sink into the sea to allow ships to pass through with strict surveillance.

Each crescent grows a different crop, one coffee and one sugar. The Fauna work as crew bosses and managers, while the Firbolg do the grunt work in the scorching heat and oppressive humidity.
## Key Groups:
The main populations include Fauna and Firbolg of all ages. In general the Fauna seem more well fed and hearty, while the Firbolg all appear malnourished and sickly. The Fauna all only wear clothes below the belt, while the Firbolg only wear clothes above the belt. They are otherwise completely nude. Each group resents the other with an intense hatred because of this.

[[Player View/Groups/The Eye of the Storm|The Eye of the Storm]] maintains a small force on this island, although it appears to mainly be to keep the Fauna and Firbolg under yoke and working the fields. (It is mainly [[Kai_DM_Notes/NPCs/Figaro & Tinafer]])

Evaporator parts can be found by either siding with the Fauns or Firbolgs, or by joining them together and overthrowing [[Kai_DM_Notes/General Notes/The Eye of the Storm|The Eye of the Storm's]] hold on [[Player View/Horny Island/Session 2/201 Horny Island|Horny Island]]. Siding with only one or the other will result in the destruction of the rejected group.
## Crops:
The Firbolg half of the island grows bananas and sugar. The bananas and sugar are harvested monthly (magical, not usual for this world), but the sugar is mostly used for El Dia del Viento, a ritual honoring Ylime, Goddess of Storms.

The Faun half of the island grows papayas and coffee beans. The papayas and coffee beans are harvested monthly (magical, not usual for this world), but the coffee beans are mostly used for El Dia del Viento, a ritual honoring Ylime, Goddess of Storms.

The Navy buys the majority of bananas and papayas from each island (secretly dealing with both) in exchange for food and other trade goods at terrible rates. They always express interest in buying the sugar and coffee beans, but are always rebuffed because the sugar and beans are needed for El Dia del Viento.
## Ylime ("ee-la-mee"), Goddess of Storms:
Frequent hurricanes occur in the middle of the bay between the two islands. A monthly ritual, El Dia del Viento, is performed to appease Ylime and convince her to place the hurricane on the other side of the bay to terrorize the devils on the other island. However, both islands perform this ritual, and thus two hurricanes appear and spin in opposite directions, causing a massive wind and water tunnel. If either island were ever to stop performing this ritual, they would anger Ylime and surely be destroyed by the opposing island's hurricane.

This Goddess and ritual were created by [[Kai_DM_Notes/General Notes/The Eye of the Storm|The Eye of the Storm]] to trick the Fauns and Firbolgs into growing and tending their fields for almost no pay.
### El Dia del Viento:
The ritual involves creating a massive pile of packaged sugar/coffee beans on the edge of the middle of the bay and slowly tossing it off into the water, one package at a time, as soon as the mists start to cover the waters of the bay and the storm begins to brew. Lots of chanting and music  accompanies this event, and it takes a good hour to complete.

Unseen and unbeknownst to the islanders is that two more rituals are taking place simultaneously in cavern alcoves directly below the sugar and coffee sacrifices and open to the bay. These are carried out by [[Kai_DM_Notes/NPCs/Figaro & Tinafer]] and their druidic understudies supplied by [[Kai_DM_Notes/General Notes/The Eye of the Storm|The Eye of the Storm]]. They start by summoning mists to shroud their work, then collect the falling merchandise to be loaded onto Navy ships to be transported and sold on other islands. As they work, [[Kai_DM_Notes/NPCs/Figaro & Tinafer]], one in each alcove, begin summoning twin hurricanes to transport the ships away from the island quickly.
